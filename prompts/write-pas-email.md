# Write PAS Email Prompt

## How to Use This Prompt

When you need to write a promotional email using the Problem-Agitate-Solve framework.

---

## The Prompt
Write a promotional email using the PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) framework.

Product/Offer: [describe]
Target audience: [describe]
Core pain point: [describe]
Desired outcome: [describe]
CTA: [what action should they take]

Structure:

Subject Line: Under 40 characters. Curiosity-based or pain-based.

Opening (Problem):

Relate to their identity. "If you're [identity] like me, you know how [pain] it is to..."
Use their exact language from customer research.
Make them think "that's exactly me."
What's In It For Me (Early):

State the promise early. "So let me show you the [solution] to [outcome]."
Agitate:

Describe the long-term cost of not fixing this.
Where does staying the same lead? (9-to-5, hiding, never making your mark...)
Make the pain worse before you solve it.
Transition:

"But that doesn't have to be you."
The Justification (One-Liner Social Proof):

One sentence that provides proof and shifts belief.
"In fact, [specific example of someone like them who succeeded]."
New Paradigm/CTA:

"If you want to [outcome], [action]: [link]."
Rules:

Keep paragraphs 1-3 lines max.
No run-on sentences.
Cut every word that doesn't earn its place.
Simple, clean copy beats clever, complex copy.
Write the email now.
---

## The Framework This Is Based On

**PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)** is the most reliable copy framework for emails. The extended version is **PASO** (add Outcome).

The agitation step is what most copy misses — and it's the most important for conversion.
