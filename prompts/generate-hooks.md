# Generate Hooks Prompt

**Tags:** #prompt #hooks #curiosity #headlines #claude

## How to Use This Prompt

When you need hooks for ads, subject lines, headlines, or content openings.

---

## The Prompt
Generate 10 hooks for [product/offer] targeting [audience] with [pain point].

Use these hook types:

Curiosity Loophole (Suby): "This is the weird [loophole/method/discovery] we found to [result]. This is not [obvious thing 1], this is not [obvious thing 2]..."
Pattern Interrupt (Brunson): Something visually or conceptually unexpected that stops the scroll.
Negative Statement: Start with what it's NOT to rule out assumptions.
Counterintuitive Claim: Something that goes against common belief.
Open Loop (Chaperon): Start a story without finishing it. "Something strange happened..."
Question That Forces Engagement: A question the prospect mentally answers.
Specific Number + Promise: "The 3 things that..." "How I [specific result] in [specific timeframe]"
Identity Hook (Chaperon): Speak directly to "Frank" — the struggling version of the prospect.
Worst Moment (Hormozi): Lead with specific pain. "Two months from shutting our doors..."
"How To" With Twist: A how-to that promises something unexpected.
For each hook, explain why it works (which psychological mechanism it uses).
---

## Hook Theory Reference

- **Curiosity gap:** Humans need to close information gaps. Open one and withhold resolution.
- **Pattern interrupt:** The brain filters out the familiar. Break the pattern to get attention.
- **Identity recognition:** When someone sees themselves in the hook ("that's me"), they can't ignore it.
- **Specificity:** Specifics are believable. Vague claims are filtered as noise.
