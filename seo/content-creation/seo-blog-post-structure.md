# How to Write an SEO-Optimized Blog Post

**Source:** HubSpot (Jamal)
**Tags:** #seo #blog #content-structure #template #hubspot

## Core Concept

Every blog post must be built for SEO from the very first step — not optimized as an afterthought. If content doesn't rank, a potential customer looking for your solution has almost no chance of finding you.

## Step 1: Keyword Research

Find and analyze search terms people actually enter, and match them to topics relevant to your business. This produces your target keyword list for the post.

## Step 2: Pre-Writing Preparation (The Outline)

Don't jump straight into writing.
- Use a template for the post type (how-to, listicle, "what is" article)
- Sketch out key points and organize them logically
- Plan supporting content (images, videos)
- Do additional research now and log every URL you'll want to link to, so you don't have to hunt for them later

## Step 3: Writing the Headline

Most professional bloggers write the headline first. A good headline checks 5 boxes:
1. Contains the keyword, preferably near the beginning
2. Highlights the benefit the reader gets from clicking
3. Includes a clear call-to-action
4. Leverages positive or negative emotion to provoke a reaction
5. Stays around 60 characters or less so it displays fully everywhere

## Step 4: Writing the Introduction

Must accomplish three things:
1. State why the subject matters to the reader — include the primary keyword in the first sentence or two (signals relevance to both reader and algorithm)
2. Address the target audience/industry and reiterate the benefit
3. Summarize what the article will cover, to entice continued scrolling

## Step 5: Writing the Body Copy

Regardless of format (list, how-to, journalistic), these rules apply:
- **Write in discreet, skimmable chunks:** 2-3 sentence thoughts. Easy for readers to skim, easy for search engines to parse.
- **Include internal and external links:** search engines favor pages that link out to relevant content and to relevant pages on your own site — it builds authority and credibility.
- **Insert secondary keywords organically:** relevant-but-not-central terms, never keyword-stuffed. Done well, this can help a single post rank for hundreds or thousands of secondary keywords, each pulling a small stream of readers.
- **Group content with keyword-rich subheads (H2/H3):** treat subheads like mini-headlines — they inform the reader AND act as "road signs" telling search engines the content is relevant.

## Step 6: Post-Writing Optimization

### Add and Optimize Images
- Visuals break up text and reinforce the message
- Fill out alt text for every image, with the same care as a title or subhead — helps image-search ranking and adds another keyword touchpoint

### Optimize for Featured Snippets
Featured snippets (paragraph, list, or table shown at the top of results) reward content written as a direct answer to a specific question or term definition.

### Finalize Meta Tags
- **Title meta tag:** the article title as shown in search results (can differ from the on-page title)
- **Meta description:** the descriptive snippet shown in results, instead of defaulting to your first few lines
- **URL:** strip down to the basic keywords for a clean link
