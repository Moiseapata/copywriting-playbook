# E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness

**Source:** Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital), Tim Cameron-Kitchen (Exposure Ninja)
**Tags:** #seo #eeat #trust #ai-search #matt-diggity #exposure-ninja

## Core Concept

Google evaluates content on Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness — with a particularly heavy weight on the "E" for first-hand **Experience**. Generative AI tools (ChatGPT, AI Overviews) evaluate content on the exact same signals when deciding what to cite or recommend.

## Signals of Real Experience

- Original photos and screenshots (not stock images)
- Case studies with real, specific numbers
- Personal anecdotes and firsthand stories

## Signals of Credibility

- Named author with a photo and detailed bio (credentials, accreditations)
- Publish date and last-updated date
- Consistent structure (pros, cons, ideal use case)

## If You Don't Have Direct Experience

Interview and feature someone who does. The credibility signal doesn't have to come from the article's author personally — it has to be present in the piece somewhere.

## E-E-A-T by Niche Type

### Health/Finance ("Your Money or Your Life" sites)
Show the article author with a link to a detailed bio including credentials and accreditations. Display publish date and next scheduled review date.

### Non-Accredited Spaces (e.g. lawn care, home services)
Demonstrate experience differently — pack a blog post with original videos from a passionate expert showing genuine first-hand experience. This makes product recommendations far more valuable than generic AI-generated advice.

### Across the Whole Website (not just blog posts)
Use E-E-A-T signals on homepages too:
- "As seen in" media logos
- Clinically-studied or certification badges
- Reviews and ratings
- Details about the specific people/experts behind the product

## Why This Matters More in the AI Era

Both traditional search engines and generative engines want to surface reputable, high-quality, experienced sources. AI tools synthesize answers based on who is mentioned most and trusted fastest — a site that's thin on E-E-A-T signals is invisible to AI recommendation engines, not just low-ranking on Google.

## How to Use

Before publishing any piece of content, audit it against this checklist:
- [ ] Named, credentialed author
- [ ] Publish/update date visible
- [ ] At least one original photo, screenshot, or data point
- [ ] A personal anecdote or case study, ideally with numbers
- [ ] Consistent structural pattern across your site's content
