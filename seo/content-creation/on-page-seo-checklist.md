# On-Page SEO Checklist

**Source:** Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital)
**Tags:** #seo #on-page #checklist #matt-diggity #template

## Core Concept

On-page SEO is how you help Google (and AI crawlers) understand and correctly rank content that's already good. It won't save weak content, but weak on-page execution will hold back genuinely great content.

## The Checklist

### Title Tag
- Include the target keyword
- Keep under 60 characters
- Make it compelling enough to click, not just descriptive

### Header Tags (H1, H2, H3)
- One H1 per page (ideally mirrors the title tag)
- H2s for main sections, H3s for subsections
- Incorporate keywords naturally — never force them

### URL Structure
- Short and descriptive
- Include the target keyword
- Example: `/platform/managed-edr`

### Meta Description
- 150-160 characters, shown under the title in results
- Not a direct ranking factor, but critical for click-through rate
- Write it like ad copy — sell the click

### Internal Linking
- Add contextual links in body copy to other relevant pages on your site
- Connects topic clusters and passes authority to money pages
- One of the best effort-to-value ratios in all of SEO

### Image Optimization
- Descriptive filenames
- Specific alt text describing the image (also serves accessibility)
- Compressed file sizes (WebP format)

## Why This Matters

Every item on this list is low-effort relative to its impact — especially internal linking, which directly channels authority toward the pages that actually convert (your money pages). See `keyword-research/keyword-sweet-spot-framework.md` for how money pages fit into the bigger prioritization picture.
