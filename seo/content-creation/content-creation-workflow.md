# Content Creation Workflow (4 Phases)

**Source:** Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital)
**Tags:** #seo #content-creation #ai-content #matt-diggity

## Core Concept

Ranking content isn't about writing more words — it's about a repeatable process that combines competitive research, structure, and a human layer AI can't replicate. Word count is not a ranking factor by itself; **comprehensiveness of topic coverage** is what counts.

## Phase 1: Research

Manually read the top 5 ranking results for your target keyword. Identify:
- What they all cover (table stakes — you must include this too)
- What they miss (your opportunity to differentiate)
- Average length (a rough benchmark, not a target)
- Unique angles already being used

This analysis can be automated with a content-scoring tool (e.g. Surfer's Content Editor), which analyzes competitor outlines to ensure exhaustive topic coverage and surfaces the "Questions" people are asking about the topic.

## Phase 2: Structure

Build a full outline before writing a single sentence of body copy. Use AI to draft a first-pass structure, then refine it manually based on your Phase 1 research.

## Phase 3: Writing (Human + AI)

- Use AI for the first draft, the structure, and basic definitions.
- Then **add what AI cannot invent**: your unique experience, opinions, original data, case studies, expert interviews, specific real examples.

**This human layer is your competitive moat in a sea of AI-generated content.** Aim for content that is "5 times better" than what currently exists — not just marginally different.

## Phase 4: Optimization

Use a content-scoring tool to quantify comprehensiveness and confirm you've covered the topic for both traditional search and AI search. Aim to beat the top score on the tool's dashboard, not just hit a passing grade.

## Why the Human Layer Matters

Pure AI content blends into the noise — readers and algorithms can tell. The unique experience, data, and stories you inject are what make content citable, trustworthy, and differentiated. See `content-creation/eeat-framework.md` for the deeper reasoning.
