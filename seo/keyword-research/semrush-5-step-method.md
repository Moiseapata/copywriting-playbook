# A Simple 5-Step Keyword Research Method (Semrush)

**Source:** Semrush
**Tags:** #seo #keyword-research #tools #semrush #template

## Core Concept

A concrete, tool-based execution of keyword research: go from a broad seed keyword to a published, optimized article using real search data — designed to find high-demand, low-competition topics rather than keywords you'll never rank for.

## Step 1: Generate Keyword Ideas (Keyword Magic Tool)

- Start with a broad **seed keyword** relevant to your niche (e.g., "vegan recipes"). Starting broad surfaces hidden sub-niches — critical in an era of AI-generated content and rising competition.
- Key metrics to understand:
  - **Search Volume:** estimated monthly searches
  - **Keyword Difficulty (KD%):** 0-100 score of how hard it is to rank
  - **Search Intent:** Informational / Transactional / Commercial / Navigational
  - **Long-Tail Keywords:** lower volume, high specificity, low competition
- Filter for quick wins: set KD% to 0-29 ("Easy"/"Very Easy"), sort by search volume descending.

## Step 2: Analyze Keywords and Build a Target List

- Open the Keyword Overview page for a chosen keyword: confirms volume, difficulty, intent.
- **Keyword Ideas section:** related keywords, variations, and real questions people search.
- **SERP Analysis section:** shows exactly what's ranking now — confirms the expected content format and any SERP features (featured snippet, image pack).
- **Page Authority Score (AS):** targeting keywords where competitors have lower AS makes ranking easier.
- Filter for "Informational" intent, select relevant non-branded keywords with decent volume, and save them to a target list.

## Step 3: Find Competitor Keyword Gaps

Use a keyword gap tool: enter your domain plus 3-4 competitor domains (pulled from Step 2's SERP analysis).

- **Missing Keywords:** high-volume keywords competitors rank for that you don't — ideal for new content.
- **Weak Keywords:** keywords where you rank lower than competitors (especially positions 11-30 / page 2) — "low-hanging fruit," perfect for optimizing *existing* content rather than writing new pages.

Add relevant missing/weak keywords to your target list from Step 2.

## Step 4: Cluster Keywords into Topic Groups

Select the full keyword list and cluster it. Grouping similar topics together (e.g., "small cookies," "mini batch chocolate chip cookies") makes it possible to rank one pillar page for a whole group of related terms, instead of writing separate thin pages for each.

## Step 5: Create a Content Brief and Draft

- Generate a content brief for a keyword cluster: select competitor articles to analyze, refine the suggested title/length, finalize secondary keywords, approve the AI-suggested outline and meta description.
- Generate an AI draft as a **starting point only** — it must be customized before publishing.

### Key Optimizations Before Publishing
- **Featured snippet bait:** a 40-60 word summary at the top of the article, directly answering the main query, formatted as a list/table where relevant.
- **FAQ section:** built from the keyword questions surfaced in research.
- **Clear, original images:** used by virtually every top-ranked competitor.
- **Firsthand experience:** the single best differentiator from AI-generated content — personal expertise Google (and readers) can't get anywhere else.
