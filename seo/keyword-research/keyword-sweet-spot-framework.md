# The Keyword Sweet Spot Framework

**Source:** Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital)
**Tags:** #seo #keyword-research #prioritization #matt-diggity

## Core Concept

Most keyword research produces a chaotic list. This framework filters that list down to the keywords actually worth your time, then sequences your content production so authority compounds instead of spreading thin.

## The Sweet Spot: 4 Filters

A keyword is worth targeting only if it passes all four:

1. **Demand:** Are people actually searching for it?
2. **Fit:** Where does it sit in the funnel (learning vs. buying)? Is it relevant to your business?
3. **Intent:** Can you actually satisfy what the searcher wants (a blog post? a video? a tool?)?
4. **Difficulty:** Can you realistically rank for it given your site's current authority?

## Keyword Research Process: 3 Disciplines

### 1. Ideation (Free Methods)
- **Google Search Console:** The single best source of real search data — queries you already get impressions for.
- **Google Auto-Suggest & "People Also Ask":** Mine real queries directly from Google's own UI.
- **Reddit:** Read how customers describe their problems in their own words.
- **AI Assistants:** Ask ChatGPT what questions someone would ask before buying your type of product. Paste your sitemap into ChatGPT to surface content gaps.
- **Browser extensions (e.g. Surfer's free Chrome extension):** See search volume and ideas directly inside Google results.

### 2. Validation
Assess each idea for search volume, difficulty, business fit, and intent. This step can be automated with keyword research tools.

### 3. Clustering
Group related keywords together. This turns a chaotic list into an organized topical map, so one piece of content can capture many related searches at once.

## The Bottom-Up Prioritization Plan

**Step 1 — Money Pages First.** Build the pages that directly convert (product, service, pricing pages) before anything else. These are the foundation everything else supports.

**Step 2 — Build One Topical Cluster at a Time.** Once a money page exists, build the full supporting funnel around it. A tightly interlinked content ecosystem on one topic signals topical authority to Google, making it easier to rank for every keyword in that cluster — not just the head term.

**Step 3 — Repeat Cluster by Cluster.** Finish building authority around one topic completely before starting the next. Spreading resources across multiple topics simultaneously delays results for all of them.

## Why This Matters

This is the keyword-research analog of the "three realities" principle: prioritization beats execution. A business with mediocre content on one dominant cluster will outrank a business with decent content spread across ten disconnected topics.
