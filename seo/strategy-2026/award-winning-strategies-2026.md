# The Best SEO Strategies for 2026: 5 Award-Nominated Approaches

**Source:** Tim Cameron-Kitchen (Exposure Ninja) — synthesized from campaigns shortlisted for 14 Global Search Awards
**Tags:** #seo #strategy #case-studies #2026 #exposure-ninja

## Core Concept

SEO is being reshaped by AI results, but massive Google traffic still exists. These five integrated strategies represent what's actually winning in real, awards-nominated campaigns right now — not theory.

## Strategy 1: The AI Search Revolution

Identify the conversational, topic-based queries that LLMs and tools like Perplexity surface. Write content the way these tools prefer: packed with first-party experience, highly detailed, high quality. Format and link-build to it so it gains visibility in Perplexity and Google's AI Overviews.

**Key tactic — Reverse-Engineering Perplexity:** when Perplexity runs background research, it exposes the underlying web searches it performs. Cover those specific sub-topics thoroughly so Perplexity's own research process surfaces and recommends your site.

**Results:**
- Lawn care e-commerce client: +120% YoY traffic despite AI-driven traffic declines elsewhere in the industry
- Global skincare client: content restructured around topic clusters and natural language → +451% organic traffic to the content section

**How to apply:** Move beyond individual keywords — think in topic clusters, with a detailed pillar piece supported by related, more specific pieces that link back to it. Getting the topics right requires understanding your audience in extreme detail (see Strategy 2).

## Strategy 2: Rethinking Audience & Keyword Research

As search gets more conversational and personalized, move beyond individual keywords toward mapping the full buyer journey.

**Buyer journey mapping:** For a mortgage client, distinct journeys were mapped for different customer types, with pillar + supporting content built for each stage. Result: organic sessions grew from 778 to over 150,000 (+20,000% YoY), then doubled again the following year.

**Psychographic personas (not demographics):** For a skincare client, six customer profiles were built around psychographics (e.g. "Skincare Newcomer"). Facebook surveys uncovered each persona's real motivations and objections (e.g. skepticism of new trends). Content was tailored accordingly — a skeptical persona gets proven-ingredient and review-heavy content instead of hype.

**Why this matters for AI:** AI tools give personalized results. Deep understanding of audience motivation lets you create content AI is more likely to surface for those specific users — e.g. a bathroom-installation client targeting children/carers of elderly relatives (early-stage researchers, heavy ChatGPT users) captured strong AI visibility by shaping the strategy around that specific persona.

**How to apply:** Map your audience into distinct profiles beyond a single "homogenous blob." Use surveys, Reddit, and customer calls to understand fears, objections, and motivations, then design content per persona.

## Strategy 3: Content-Driven Commerce

A blog post with a CTA at the bottom is no longer enough — AI cannibalizes informational traffic, so you have to extract more value from the traffic you do get by embedding commercial actions directly inside content.

**Examples:**
- Lawn care client: products embedded in blog posts so visitors can add to cart directly from the content page
- Skincare client: "buy" actions embedded inside routine/ingredient content, which lends itself naturally to product links
- Result: +8.5% e-commerce revenue lift — a large figure for a business of that size

**How to apply:**
- E-commerce: embed product info and add-to-cart directly in informational content
- Lead gen: embed inquiry/quote/download CTAs
- **Key principle:** the CTA must be directly relevant to the informational content around it. A user who just learned something valuable is far more likely to act.

## Strategy 4: Tapping Into Seasonality

Seasonality is a source of momentum, not a weakness. Four seasonal content strategies were planned for a lawn care client based on trend data showing when people search for different things — keyword performance tracked separately by season so content can be published or boosted in the weeks *before* a topic trends.

**Bonus benefit:** publishing right as interest spikes can also earn Google Discover visibility — one piece "blew up" on Discover because it published days before its topic gained mass attention. Result: the lawn care site now ranks top-3 for 4,874 different keywords.

**How to apply:** Challenge the assumption that your business has no seasonality — think about universal drivers (tax year-end, summer holidays, Christmas, weather changes). Build a strategy once and roll it out year after year.

## Strategy 5: Trust & Authority Boosting

Publishing generic information is pointless in the AI era — lean into authority, first-party experience, and unique in-house expertise.

- **Personal attribution & backstory:** for a "your money or your life" niche (bathroom installation for the elderly), content was attributed to specific named people with their backstory shared, building trust with both users and AI.
- **Owning the data (thought leadership):** an original survey quantified the scale of the customers' core problem. The business now "owns" these statistics — when AI or search engines are asked about the scale of this problem, the client is the reference source.
- **Cause-based authority:** a Change.org petition to the UK government on the relevant issue built authority and connection with customers.
- **Useful tools & resources:** a mortgage client built eligibility calculators and ebooks to demonstrate credibility.
- **Result:** the mortgage client ranked 69,000 keywords in 12 months (+30,000% content increase), as search engines began treating them as a genuine thought leader.

**How to apply:** Identify the insight your business has that nobody else — including AI — possesses. What data could you own? Becoming the definitive source in your market means journalists and AI cite you for years.

## The Integration Effect

The real power comes from combining these strategies with other marketing disciplines, not picking just one:
- Skincare client: SEO/content integrated with CRO work on a hero product page
- Mortgage client: SEO combined with a segmented email strategy based on journey stage
- Bathroom client: paid search improvements alongside SEO → +50% YoY lead volume
