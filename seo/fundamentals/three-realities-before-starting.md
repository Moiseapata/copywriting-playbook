# Three Realities to Accept Before Starting SEO

**Source:** Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital)
**Tags:** #seo #fundamentals #expectations #matt-diggity

## Core Concept

Nobody can guarantee a #1 ranking on Google — it's outside any single marketer's control. The goal is to stack as many favorable conditions as possible to maximize your odds. Before committing resources, set realistic expectations with these three truths.

## Reality 1: Ultra-Competitive Keywords Are Rarely Worth It

Trying to dethrone established players who've dominated a keyword for years with millions of dollars of investment is rarely a profitable use of resources for small/medium businesses. Pick battles you can actually win.

## Reality 2: Reaching #1 Is Only Half the Battle

Once you're #1, you become a target. Competitors will come for your spot. Maintaining the position requires:
- Constant content refreshing
- Continuous authority building (backlinks, topical depth)

Ranking is not a one-time project — it's ongoing maintenance.

## Reality 3: You Don't Need to Rank #1 for Everything

Wanting to rank #1 for thousands of keywords wastes resources. **The real challenge in SEO is prioritization, not execution.** Most businesses fail not because they can't execute SEO tactics, but because they spread effort across too many keywords instead of concentrating on the ones that matter to the business.

## How to Use This

When planning an SEO strategy, resist the instinct to chase every keyword. Instead:
1. Identify the handful of keywords/pages that directly drive revenue (money pages)
2. Build depth and authority around those first
3. Only expand once a cluster is genuinely dominant
