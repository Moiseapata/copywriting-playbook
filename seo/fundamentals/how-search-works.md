# How Search Works (Crawling, Indexing, Ranking)

**Source:** Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital), Neil Patel
**Tags:** #seo #fundamentals #google #ai-search #matt-diggity #neil-patel

## Core Concept

Before optimizing anything, you need the mental model of how a search engine actually decides what to show. This is also the foundation AI assistants build on — if you're invisible to Google, you're invisible to AI.

## The Three-Step Process

1. **Crawling:** Google's bots follow links to read pages across the internet.
2. **Indexing:** Google stores what it finds in a massive library (its index). A page not in the index cannot show up in results, no matter how good it is.
3. **Ranking:** When someone searches, Google selects the most relevant, trustworthy, and useful pages from its index and orders them.

## The Modern SERP (2026)

A search results page is no longer just 10 blue links. It now contains:
- AI Overview (Google's AI-generated summary)
- Sponsored ads
- Organic results (earned through SEO)
- "People Also Ask" boxes
- Video carousels, local map packs, knowledge panels
- Reddit threads, listicles, YouTube videos

**Implication:** Decode what Google believes the *searcher's intent* is by studying the mix of results — that mix IS Google's answer to "what does this person want?"

## SEO and AI Are Not Competitors

- AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity) are built on traditional search infrastructure. ChatGPT uses Bing's index; Perplexity crawls the same pages that rank in traditional search.
- **Core principle:** If your site doesn't show up in Google or Bing, it's likely invisible to AI assistants. SEO is the foundation AI search is built on, not a separate discipline.
- Google still processes ~13.7 billion searches a day and sends 190x more traffic to websites than ChatGPT. Google is not shrinking — the *definition* of search is expanding.

## Modern Definition of SEO

Historically "Search Engine Optimization," now better understood as **"Search Everywhere Optimization"**: getting your business to show up wherever people search for things related to it — Google, Bing, ChatGPT, Perplexity, YouTube, Reddit, Amazon, TikTok.

## Business Impact

The ultimate goal of SEO is always a business goal (customers, revenue, leads) — never an SEO goal in isolation (rankings, traffic) for its own sake. SEO's unique value is that it reaches people with high intent, at the exact moment they're actively looking for a solution — unlike interruptive ads.
