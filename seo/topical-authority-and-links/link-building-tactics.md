# Link Building Tactics

**Source:** Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital)
**Tags:** #seo #link-building #backlinks #matt-diggity

## Core Concept

Backlinks are "votes of confidence" from other websites — a link from a reputable site signals trustworthiness to Google. It's widely considered the hardest part of SEO, which is exactly why most beginners should delay it until they have something worth linking to.

## Practical Tactics

### 1. Be the Source
Create original data, analysis, or definitive resources (guides, studies) that journalists and bloggers will naturally want to cite. This is the highest-leverage tactic long-term but requires upfront investment.

### 2. Journalist Query Platforms
Use free platforms (e.g., Source of Sources, built by the founder of HARO) to monitor requests from journalists needing expert sources. Respond with a genuinely useful quote and earn a link when the article publishes. **This is the single most accessible tactic for beginners** — no existing authority required, just 15 minutes a day of consistent responses.

### 3. Create Linkable Assets
Build free tools, calculators, or templates. With AI, even non-technical users can now create simple browser-based tools. These become reference material that naturally attracts backlinks over time.

## Beginner Sequencing

Do not obsess over link building initially. The order matters:
1. Create 10-15 genuinely great pieces of content first (nothing to link to = no reason for backlinks)
2. Start the journalist-query tactic for 15 minutes a day
3. Let content quality do the heavy lifting before chasing links aggressively

## Technical SEO (Supporting Foundation)

- For most small-to-medium businesses on WordPress, Shopify, or Webflow, the platform itself handles most technical SEO.
- **Action:** Run Screaming Frog SEO Spider (free up to 500 URLs) to get a prioritized list of technical issues (broken links, missing title tags, etc.) and fix them.
- **Local SEO:** If you have a physical location, the single most important action is claiming and fully optimizing your Google Business Profile, plus actively getting and responding to reviews (see `topical-authority-and-links/local-seo-entity-system.md`).
