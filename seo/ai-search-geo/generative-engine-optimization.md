# Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

**Source:** Tim Cameron-Kitchen (Exposure Ninja), Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital)
**Tags:** #seo #geo #ai-search #generative-ai #exposure-ninja #matt-diggity

## Core Concept

Generative Engine Optimization is the set of techniques required to get a business featured and recommended by generative AI tools — ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overviews. It's the biggest shift in search in decades: AI-generated results are pushing traditional organic listings further down the page, and the techniques to get featured there are subtly different from classic ranking techniques.

## Scope of GEO

- Google's AI Overviews (used by over a billion people as of late 2024)
- Standalone AI tools like ChatGPT, which happily recommend specific brands and agencies by name
- Learning and implementing GEO now is a genuine first-mover advantage — most competitors haven't started

## How to Do GEO

### 1. Rank in Regular Web Search First
Most AI tools compile answers using an underlying web search. You must already rank well in traditional results to be eligible for the AI Overview. Having a featured snippet in Google makes you eligible for the AI Overview on that query.

### 2. Publish Specific, Long-Tail Content
AI Overviews and generative search encourage much longer, more specific queries. Publishing highly specific content improves your odds of ranking for those long-tail queries — and of informing the AI tool during its background sub-searches.

### 3. Match Your Content Structure to the AI Answer
Mirror the structure of the AI-generated answer: give a concise, direct answer immediately at the start, then go deeper into supporting detail. This helps Google corroborate the AI Overview's summary with your actual page content.

### 4. Demonstrate First-Hand Experience
AI tools give preference to sites that show genuine, first-hand experience of the topic — see `content-creation/eeat-framework.md`.

## The Digital PR Angle for AI

For many brands, most ongoing SEO work will shift toward: running searches on AI tools like Perplexity, identifying which third-party sites the AI is pulling from to compile its answer, and then getting your brand featured prominently on *those* sites. Digital PR and content marketing increase the odds an AI tool recommends you, even when the recommendation doesn't come directly from your own website. Branding and messaging consistency matter more here, so AI tools receive a coherent story about your product from every source.

## Reverse-Engineering Perplexity (Practical Tactic)

When Perplexity runs its background research for a query, it shows the underlying web searches it performed. You can identify these specific sub-topics and make sure your content thoroughly covers them — so when Perplexity runs those searches again, it finds and recommends your site as a source.

## The Bottom Line

Any SEO strategy for the next 12 months that doesn't include GEO is deficient. AI Overviews are not the future of search — they are the present.
