# The Split SEO Strategy & Tracking AI Visibility

**Source:** Tim Cameron-Kitchen (Exposure Ninja), Matt Diggity (Kenyon Digital)
**Tags:** #seo #ai-search #funnel #tracking #exposure-ninja #matt-diggity

## Core Concept

AI lets users complete their top-of-funnel research entirely inside the search interface — meaning organic traffic can drop even when your SEO is objectively fantastic. This requires splitting what used to be one SEO strategy into two distinct ones, and building a new measurement habit since Google Search Console can't see AI-assistant traffic.

## The Problem

**Old model:** One SEO strategy drove traffic at every funnel stage (top, middle, bottom) through to your website for research and eventual purchase.

**New model:** Top-of-funnel research can be completed without visiting any website at all. Example: a user compares business bank account features entirely inside an AI Overview and never visits a single bank's site — their next click goes straight to purchase.

## The Two Distinct Strategies Needed

### 1. Top/Middle-of-Funnel Strategy
Goal: get your brand or product **recommended inside the AI-generated answer itself.** You're fighting for visibility within the summary, not just for a click.

### 2. Bottom-of-Funnel Strategy
Goal: capture traffic from people who are now ready to buy and are searching for a specific product/brand — get them directly to your site to complete the purchase.

**Implication:** website content is still valuable, but only if it's structured to be substantially more valuable than the generic AI answer someone already saw.

## New Search Engines to Track

ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Copilot, Claude, Grok. Users increasingly ask AI assistants directly for recommendations, making visibility here a genuinely new channel — not a niche one.

## How AI Chooses Sources

AI assistants synthesize answers based on authority, relevance, and clear structure, then cite sources using largely the same fundamentals as Google's ranking algorithm.

## The Tracking Problem

Google Search Console cannot see traffic originating from AI assistants. You have to actively monitor whether your brand appears in AI-generated answers rather than assuming your existing analytics tell the whole story.

**Action — AI Tracker:** use a dedicated AI-visibility tracker to see which brands get recommended for queries in your space, and where competitors appear but you don't.

## Earning Citations: The "Mention Gap" Tactic

Every backlink signals to AI that your content is worth citing — AI especially favors "best of" roundup articles. Use a mention-gap report to find articles where competitors are cited and you are not, then build a prioritized outreach list to close those gaps.
