# Search Everywhere Optimization (SEO 2.0)

**Source:** Neil Patel
**Tags:** #seo #ai-search #cross-platform #trust-network #neil-patel

## Core Concept

Consumers no longer "search" in the traditional sense — they **decide**, in fragmented moments scattered across TikTok comments, Reddit threads, ChatGPT answers, and Amazon reviews. Optimizing only for Google rankings without understanding how decisions actually happen today doesn't just put you behind — it makes you invisible at the moment that matters.

## The Google Trap

Most businesses still play a Google-only game that effectively ended a few years ago — obsessing over rankings, meta descriptions, and backlinks. But even winning on Google can mean losing customers.

**The data:**
- Google handles ~13.7 billion searches a day
- That's only **27%** of all search-like activity on the internet
- The other **73%** is scattered across Instagram, TikTok, Amazon, Reddit, YouTube, and ChatGPT — platforms most businesses don't treat as search engines at all

**The result:** while you fight for a #1 Google ranking, customers are deciding on TikTok, validating on Reddit, asking ChatGPT for recommendations, and checking Amazon reviews. Traffic can look fine while conversions stagnate, because you're showing up in *search* but missing the *decision*.

## The New Consumer Journey: A Constellation, Not a Funnel

The modern journey is a constellation of micro-decisions happening simultaneously, each platform serving a different psychological function:

| Platform | Decision Function |
|---|---|
| Google | What to click |
| Reddit / Reviews | What to trust |
| Amazon / TikTok Shop | What to buy |
| App Store Ratings | What to try |
| YouTube / Podcasts | What to think |
| ChatGPT / Claude / AI models | What to believe |
| Instagram / LinkedIn | Who to follow |
| AI (everywhere) | Who to cite / reference |

A single purchase journey might involve seeing a product on TikTok, checking Amazon reviews, validating it on Reddit, asking ChatGPT for alternatives, then buying — without ever visiting the brand's website.

## The New Playbook

SEO isn't dead, it's expanded. Traditional SEO got you found on Google; **Search Everywhere Optimization gets you chosen across the entire internet.** When someone asks ChatGPT for a recommendation, or checks Reddit, your brand needs to already be part of that conversation — these platforms don't just influence the decision, they *are* the decision.

## Platform-Specific "Decision Codes"

Using the same content everywhere is a common mistake — each platform has its own psychology:

| Platform | Decision Driver | Content Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok | Emotion & novelty | Immediate, visual, emotionally resonant — people want to feel, not think |
| YouTube | Retention & perceived expertise | Depth, authority, proof — people come to learn and evaluate |
| ChatGPT / AI | Citations & semantic clarity | Clear, factual, authoritative — flashy visuals are irrelevant |
| Amazon | Social proof & trust | Reviews matter more than product descriptions |
| Instagram | Aspirational identity | Content sells a lifestyle/identity, not just a product |
| Reddit | Raw authenticity | Honest, unfiltered opinions — any marketing tone is rejected |

## Visibility vs. Validation

- **Visibility** = showing up (having an account, ranking on Google) — what *you* do.
- **Validation** = being mentioned by others (referenced in someone's TikTok, cited by ChatGPT) — what *others* say about you.

**Why validation matters more:** AI doesn't scroll results like a human — it summarizes based on who is mentioned most and trusted fastest. If your brand isn't part of that validation network (Reddit threads, articles, Amazon reviews, podcasts), it effectively doesn't exist in AI's decision-making process.

## The RICE Prioritization Framework

You don't need to be everywhere — you need to be trusted somewhere that matters. Score each candidate platform 1-10 on each criterion, multiply by reach, and prioritize the top 2-3:

| Letter | Criterion | Question |
|---|---|---|
| R | Reach | How many people search on this platform daily? |
| I | Impact | How much business impact could this have? |
| C | Confidence | How confident are you that you can succeed here? |
| E | Ease | How easy is it for you to execute? |

## The Compounding Effect

Strategic presence on a few key platforms compounds automatically:
- A popular Reddit mention gets indexed by Google
- A ChatGPT citation reinforces authority everywhere else
- Dominant Amazon reviews influence decisions that started on TikTok

## First Step

Pick one platform outside Google — the one where your customers most likely validate decisions — and focus on earning trust there before expanding. AI/LLM optimization (training models to choose your brand) is currently the biggest under-exploited opportunity.
