# Sales Page Template

**Tags:** #template #landing-page #sales-page #conversion

## Structure

A short-form sales page for lower-priced offers (under $100). Longer pages are needed for higher-ticket items.

---

## Section 1: Above the Fold

**Headline:** [Clear core promise — what they get]
*Example: "A Year of Money-Making Emails Made Easy"*

**Subheadline (optional):** [Supporting promise or price]
*Example: "365 Day Inbox Income — just $27"*

**Hero Element:** Product mockup, video thumbnail, or hero image

**CTA Button:** [Direct action]
*Example: "Get Instant Access" or "Buy Now — $27"*

**Trust Element:** Small text near button — "30-day money-back guarantee"

---

## Section 2: The Intro (Why This Matters)

**Opening:** Why [topic] matters for [outcome they want].

**The Problem:** What they're currently struggling with.

**The Promise:** How this product solves it.

---

## Section 3: Objection Handling

**Headline:** "I know what you might be thinking..."

Address 2-3 common objections directly:
- "I've tried things like this before..."
- "I don't have time..."
- "Will this really work for me?"

Answer each with empathy and evidence.

---

## Section 4: The Mechanism (How It Works)

**Headline:** "Here's How [Product Name] Makes This Easy"

Explain the core mechanism simply. What's the unique approach?

---

## Section 5: Social Proof

**Testimonials:** 2-3 specific, result-focused quotes with names and photos if possible.

**Numbers:** "Join [X]+ students/customers"

**Authority:** Brief bio or credibility statement

---

## Section 6: What's Included

**Headline:** "Here's Everything You Get:"

**Checklist format:**
- [Component 1 — benefit-driven description]
- [Component 2 — benefit-driven description]
- [Component 3 — benefit-driven description]
- [Bonus 1 — with value]
- [Bonus 2 — with value]

**Total Value:** $XXX
**Today's Price:** $XX

---

## Section 7: FAQ

3-5 common questions with direct answers:
- "Is this for beginners?"
- "How long do I have access?"
- "What if it doesn't work for me?"
- "How is this different from [competitor/alternative]?"

---

## Section 8: Final CTA

**Headline:** "Ready to [outcome]?"

**CTA Button:** [Same as above]

**Final Trust Element:** Guarantee reminder, testimonial, or last piece of social proof.

---

## Key Insight (Alex Cattoni)

About 60% of page viewers visited her sales page twice before buying. People take longer to make buying decisions. Don't expect one visit to convert. Build trust and give them reasons to return.
