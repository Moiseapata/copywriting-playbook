# Re-Engagement Sequence Template

**Tags:** #template #email #re-engagement #list-hygiene

## Purpose

Re-engage inactive subscribers. Clean your list. Protect deliverability.

---

### Email 1: The Check-In

**Subject Line:** Are you still interested in [topic]?

**Body:**

> Hey [First Name],
>
> I noticed you haven't opened my emails in a while.
>
> No hard feelings — I know inboxes get overwhelming.
>
> If you still want to hear from me about [topics], just click the link below:
>
> [Link: "Yes, keep me on the list"]
>
> If I don't hear from you, I'll assume you've moved on and I'll stop emailing you in a few days.
>
> Either way, thanks for being part of this journey.
>
> [Name]

---

### Email 2: The Follow-Up (3 Days Later)

**Subject Line:** Last chance — stay or go?

**Body:**

> [First Name],
>
> Quick follow-up. I'll be cleaning my email list soon.
>
> If you want to stay, click here: [Link]
>
> If not, no worries at all. I wish you the best.
>
> [Name]

---

### Email 3: The Goodbye (3 Days Later)

**Subject Line:** Goodbye (for now)

**Body:**

> [First Name],
>
> This is my last email to you.
>
> I've removed you from my list as you requested (by not responding).
>
> If you ever want to reconnect, you can always re-subscribe at [link].
>
> Wishing you all the best,
> [Name]

---

## After the Sequence

- Tag non-responders as "Inactive"
- Suppress them from future sends
- This protects your deliverability for engaged subscribers
- They can always re-subscribe if they want back in

## Why This Matters (Hormozi)

- Unsubscribes are not an insult
- Dormant subscribers lower domain deliverability
- Lower deliverability means interested people don't receive your emails
- "Shaking the tree" pulls forward churn and protects your sender reputation
