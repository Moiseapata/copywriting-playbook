# Value Debt DM Sequence Template

**Tags:** #template #dm #outreach #value-debt

## The Sequence

A value-first DM approach based on Hormozi's value debt principle.

---

### Step 1: Pre-Outreach Engagement

**Before DMing:**
- Follow the person
- Engage genuinely with 3-5 of their posts (meaningful comments, not "great post!")
- Let them see your name before you slide into DMs

---

### Step 2: The Value-First DM

**Template:**

> Hey [Name] — I've been following your content on [topic]. Really liked your take on [specific thing they posted].
>
> I actually put together a [resource] on [related topic] that I think you'd find useful. It covers [specific benefit].
>
> Want me to send it over? No pitch, just thought you'd appreciate it.

---

### Step 3: Deliver the Value

**When they say yes:**

> Here you go: [link]
>
> The part on [specific section] might be most relevant to what you're working on.
>
> Let me know if anything stands out — would love to hear your thoughts.

---

### Step 4: Follow-Up (2-3 Days Later)

**Template:**

> Hey — did you get a chance to check out that [resource]? Curious if [specific section] was helpful for you.

---

### Step 5: The Natural Transition (Only If They Engaged)

**If they respond positively:**

> Glad it was helpful! I actually help [target audience] implement this exact approach.
>
> If you ever want to explore what that would look like for [their specific situation], happy to chat. No pressure either way.
>
> Either way, keep up the great content.

---

## Key Principles

- **Give first.** The value must be genuine and better than most paid stuff.
- **No pitch in the first DM.** Build value debt before any ask.
- **Make it easy to say yes.** "No pressure" and "happy to chat" are low-friction.
- **Respect the "no."** If they don't engage, don't push. Continue providing value publicly.

## The "Learning Stage" Pricing Ramp (Hormozi)

When starting with outreach:
1. Free (first 5 clients — build case studies)
2. 80% off (next 5)
3. 60% off (next 5)
4. Raise prices every 5 customers until at market rate
