# 6 Components of a Landing Page That Converts

**Source:** Alex Cattoni
**Tags:** #landing-pages #conversion #copy-components #alex-cattoni

## Core Principle

Your landing page is the single most important part of your sales funnel. If you double your conversion rate, you double your leads and can double your business.

## The 6 Components

### 1. A Free, High-Value Promise

**Rule:** Give what you would want to receive.

Your landing page gives your brand a one-shot opportunity to demonstrate value and earn trust from day one.

**Requirements:**
- Communicate a clear, singular benefit
- Make it good without being hypey
- Answer "What's in it for me?" immediately

**Key Insight:** Your leads expect something genuinely useful. Make it something they'd actually consider paying for. Deliver on the promise immediately.

### 2. A Strong "Reason Why"

Even when you offer something for free, answer the inner skeptic.

**The question in their mind:** "If this is so valuable, why are you giving it away for free?"

**Best answers are simple:**
- "I want you to experience my work, because once you do, you'll be hooked"
- "The feedback is so positive I wanted as many people as possible to benefit"

**Scarcity (use carefully):**
- Limited time availability
- First 100 get a bonus
- Only use REAL scarcity—never fake it

### 3. Authentic Social Proof

People look to others for cues on what to think, feel, and do.

**Types to use:**
- Testimonials (with permission)
- Audience size
- Recent comments
- Screenshots of positive DMs
- "As Seen On" with logos

**Placement:** Throughout the page, especially right before the offer or CTA.

### 4. Trust Factors

Write copy first, then design around it, working in trust elements:
- Privacy promise on opt-in form
- Clear statement about email usage
- Reminder they can unsubscribe anytime
- Secure domain indicators
- Third-party trust seals

**Goal:** Reduce friction. Make people feel safe.

### 5. Crystal-Clear CTA

You have 5-10 seconds to convince visitors to stay. The offer and action must be clear immediately.

**Rules:**
- Front and center
- No confusion about what to do next
- Easy to spot and easy to use

**Multi-Step Tip:** Use micro-commitments (Cialdini's principle). Start with a button, then a popup, then ask for email. Each step is a small "yes."

### 6. Brand Consistency

**The biggest blind spot in marketing.**

There must be clear overlap and congruence across every touchpoint leading to the landing page. If your ad promises one thing but the page delivers something else, you lose them immediately.

**Rule:** Your brand needs a distinct, memorable voice that stays consistent everywhere.

## Concise Execution

Many of these components can be covered with just a few words. Like any copy, the key is to be concise and absolutely clear. As Einstein said: "If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."
