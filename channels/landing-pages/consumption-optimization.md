# Consumption Optimization

**Source:** Sabri Suby
**Tags:** #landing-pages #design #consumption #suby

## Core Principle

If people aren't consuming the information, it doesn't matter how compelling it is. The design must be frictionless and optimized for reading—not for looking like a "funnel."

## The Problem

Most funnels look like a cluttered "Vegas Strip":
- Countdown timers everywhere
- 10 different font colors
- Busy navigation
- Price tag immediately visible
- Flashing elements

**Result:** The page screams "I'M SELLING YOU SOMETHING" and triggers defensive behavior.

## The Fix

### Strip It Down
- Black text on white background
- Remove ALL navigation
- No fake scarcity timers
- No distracting background images
- Single, central line of focus for the eye

### The Goal
The page should look like a regular email or article, not a desperate sales flyer. This signals human-to-human interaction and keeps you out of the mental "spam folder."

## The Hero Section Rules

### Curiosity Over Literal Headlines

**Wrong:** "Learn How to Make Money with Local Reviews"
**Why it fails:** The prospect thinks they already know the answer, so they bounce.

**Right:** "This is the weird loophole we found to help local businesses make extra money. This is not Facebook ads, this is not Instagram..."

**Why it works:** Creates curiosity, rules out obvious answers, promises a reveal.

## The Button Delay (Suby's Biggest Split Test Winner)

Add a short time delay before the CTA button appears. This forces visitors to consume at least some of the page content before they can act.

**Result:** Higher conversion because people are more informed when they click.

## Design Checklist

- [ ] No navigation menu
- [ ] Single column layout
- [ ] Black text on white/light background
- [ ] One clear visual hierarchy
- [ ] CTA button is the most obvious element
- [ ] No fake urgency elements
- [ ] Looks like content, not advertising
