# Button Delay Pop

**Source:** Sabri Suby
**Tags:** #landing-pages #CTA #conversion #suby

## The Tactic

Add a short time delay before the main CTA button appears on a landing page.

## Why It Works

When the CTA button is immediately visible:
- Visitors click before consuming any content
- They arrive at the next step uninformed
- Conversion drops because they're not pre-sold

When the CTA is delayed:
- Visitors are forced to read at least some of the page
- They understand the offer before acting
- The click is more intentional and qualified

## Implementation

- Delay the button by 15-30 seconds
- Or trigger it on scroll (e.g., after 50% of page viewed)
- The button can be a pop-up or simply appear in-place

## Result

Suby reports this was one of his biggest split-test winners across multiple campaigns.

## Related Concept: Micro-Commitments

Instead of putting the full opt-in form on the page:
1. Start with a button: "Get the free guide"
2. That opens a popup with the form
3. Each step is a small "yes" that builds momentum

Based on Cialdini's principle of commitment and consistency: once someone takes a small action, they're more likely to take a larger one.
