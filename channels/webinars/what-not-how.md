# The "What, Not How" Rule

**Source:** Russell Brunson
**Tags:** #webinars #teaching #brunson

## Core Concept

This is where most webinars fail. You must teach the strategy (the "what"), not the tactics (the "how").

## The Distinction

### The "What" (Strategy — Teach This)
- The overall framework
- The principles behind it
- Why it works
- The steps in the process
- The outcomes they can expect

### The "How" (Tactics — Sell This)
- Which buttons to click
- Exact software settings
- Specific implementation details
- The granular execution

## Why Teaching "How" Kills Sales

If you teach the tactical "how":
- You overwhelm the audience
- They feel they have enough to try it on their own
- They leave thinking "I can figure this out myself"
- They don't need your paid product

## What to Do Instead

1. **Teach the framework:** Show them WHAT to do and WHY
2. **Demonstrate results:** Show the outcome when it's done right
3. **Withhold implementation:** The paid course is where they get the exact steps

## The Formula

"Here's the strategy that works. Here's proof it works. Here's what happens when you implement it. If you want the exact implementation roadmap, that's what my course/coaching gives you."

## Webinar Benchmarks (Paid Traffic)

- **Registration Rate:** 30-50% of page visitors
- **Show-Up Rate:** 20-30% of registrants
- **Close Rate (of attendees):**
  - 5% = six-figure year
  - 10% = multi-million dollar year
  - 15% = billion-dollar trajectory
