# The 3-Person Survey

**Source:** Russell Brunson
**Tags:** #webinars #engagement #brunson

## The Tactic

In the first 5 minutes of a webinar, run this interaction:

"Before we dive in, I want to know who's in the room. In the chat, type:
- '1' if you're a complete beginner
- '2' if you have some experience
- '3' if you're advanced"

## Why the First 5 Minutes Matter

This is the biggest drop-off point in any webinar. If people don't feel the webinar is for them, they leave immediately.

## What to Do After the Survey

Acknowledge each group specifically:

"For the '1's — you're in the right place. This will give you the foundation you need without overwhelming you."

"For the '2's — you've probably tried some things that didn't work. Today I'll show you why and what to do instead."

"For the '3's — you already know the basics. Today I'll share the nuances that took me from good to great."

## Why This Works

- Every segment feels seen and included
- No one thinks "this isn't for me"
- It creates immediate interaction (engagement signal)
- It gives you audience data for your pitch
- It reduces the psychological distance between you and the audience

## The Deeper Psychology

People are asking themselves: "Am I in the right room?"

If you don't answer that question explicitly, they'll answer it themselves—often with "no" and a click away.

## Variation for Different Formats

- **Email:** "Hit reply and tell me: are you just starting out, or have you been at this a while?"
- **DM:** "Quick question — where are you at with [topic]? Beginner, intermediate, or advanced?"
- **Sales page:** "This is for you if..." / "This is NOT for you if..."
