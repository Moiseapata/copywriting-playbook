# PAS Framework (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

**Source:** Tyson 4D, multiple
**Tags:** #email #copy-formula #pas #tyson-4d

## Core Concept

PAS is the most reliable copy framework for emails. It follows the natural psychological sequence of making someone feel their problem, intensifying the pain, then presenting the solution.

## The Framework

### P — Problem
Relate to a core pain point in their language.

**Example:** "Every year, more men report higher levels of loneliness."

**Rules:**
- Use their exact words
- Be specific, not general
- Make them think "that's exactly me"

### A — Agitate
Describe the long-term negative consequences of not fixing it. Make the pain worse.

**Example:** "It's estimated 90% of men will die without a partner. But I won't let that be you."

**Rules:**
- Don't just repeat the problem—intensify it
- Show the cost of inaction
- Make the future feel urgent

### S — Solve
Present your product/service as the natural solution.

**Example:** "That's exactly what I can help you do. If you want to attract your dream woman with a few small tweaks..."

**Rules:**
- Position the solution as the logical next step
- Don't oversell—the agitation did the work
- Make the CTA clear and low-friction

## The Extended Version: PASO

### PASO — Add Outcome
After the solve, paint the desired outcome vividly.

**Example:** "Click here to schedule a free call and find out how."

## When to Use PAS

- Promotional emails
- Landing page body copy
- Sales page openings
- Video scripts
- DM sequences

## Why PAS Works

It follows the natural decision sequence:
1. I have a problem (awareness)
2. This problem is worse than I thought (urgency)
3. Here's a solution that makes sense (action)

The agitation step is what most copy misses—and it's the most important for conversion.
