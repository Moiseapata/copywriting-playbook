# Subject Line Formulas

**Source:** Alchemy Worx study (21 billion emails analyzed), Tyson 4D
**Tags:** #email #subject-lines #curiosity #data

## The Data

Alchemy Worx analyzed 21 billion marketing emails from 2,500+ brands to identify what drives open rates.

## Three Elements That Influence Opens (Ranked)

1. **Sender name** (most important—people respond to relationships)
2. **Subject line**
3. **Preview text**

## Best Performing Words

| Word | Lift |
|------|------|
| Upgrade | 65% |
| Just | 64% |
| Content | 59% |
| Go | 55% |
| Wonderful | 55% |
| Video | +13% |
| Numbers (7, 10, etc.) | +57% |
| Free | +10% |
| News | +15% |
| Last chance | +9% |

## Words to Avoid (Spam Triggers)

Avoid overly promotional or spammy language. Use tools like spamanalyze.com to check before sending.

## 7 Subject Line Types (With Psychology)

### 1. Curiosity
**Psychology:** Humans dislike incomplete information (open loops)
**Examples:**
- "Don't open this email"
- "What they eat in prison"
- "I said I'd never do this..."
- "Can you eat yourself thin?"

### 2. Questions
**Psychology:** Questions force the brain to engage
**Examples:**
- "Are you joining us?"
- "Quick question..."
- "Did you see this?"

### 3. Drama
**Psychology:** Emotional tension captures attention
**Examples:**
- "I was arrested at the airport"
- "My confession"
- "This is embarrassing"
- "Bad news"
- "Goodbye"

### 4. Personalization
**Psychology:** Relevance and familiarity increase engagement (+14% opens)
**Examples:**
- "Hey John"
- "John, check this out"
- "Happy birthday, John"

### 5. How-To
**Psychology:** Promise of outcome or solution
**Examples:**
- "How to get 1,000 followers tonight"
- "How to buy a house for $1"

### 6. Cold Outreach
**Psychology:** Curiosity + relevance
**Examples:**
- "Quick question"
- "Ecommerce copywriter for [Company]"
- "Are you ready?"

### 7. Sales & Promotions (Sequences)
**Psychology:** Repetition builds familiarity; urgency drives action
**Sequence:** Big announcement → FAQs → Case studies → Reminder → Last chance

## Best Practices

- Keep under 9 words (mobile-friendly)
- Avoid excessive special characters
- Use emojis sparingly (typically at the end)
- Never use clickbait (don't mislead)
- AB test fundamentally different angles (pain-based vs. promise-based)
