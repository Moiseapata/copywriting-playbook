# Welcome Sequences

**Source:** Alex Hormozi, Amy Porterfield, Tyson 4D
**Tags:** #email #welcome #onboarding #engagement

## Core Principle

The welcome sequence is your highest-engagement opportunity. Someone just opted in—they're interested and paying attention. Don't waste this moment by just delivering the lead magnet.

## Hormozi's Welcome Strategy

### Step 1: Sell the Subscription, Not Just the Magnet
Most people opt in, grab the freebie, and leave. You must sell them on ongoing communication BEFORE giving the magnet.

### Step 2: The "Peel Out" Method
When creating content (like a book), some great sections don't fit the final flow. Finish them to full quality and "peel" them out as lead magnets.

### Step 3: Force Engagement (The Reply)
Require them to reply to receive the promised item. A reply signals to email servers that the interaction is highly engaging, improving deliverability.

**Example:** "Reply YES to get the secret chapter on how to make your advertising 9.5x as profitable."

## Porterfield's Delivery Rule

**Never deliver the freebie directly on the thank-you page.**

The goal is to train your audience to open and value your emails. The thank-you page should guide them to their inbox: "Go check your email!"

When you eventually launch a paid product, they'll be conditioned to open your sales emails.

## Standard Welcome Sequence Structure

### Email 1 (Immediate): Welcome + Deliver
- Set expectations ("You'll get 3 emails a week on learning piano")
- Deliver the lead magnet
- Tell them what's coming next

### Email 2 (Day 2-3): Pure Value
- Best content, no pitch
- Establishes you deliver on promises
- Builds trust

### Email 3 (Day 4-5): Value + Soft CTA
- Another value piece
- Soft mention of a product/service
- "P.S. I have a product if you're interested, no worries if not"

## Key Metrics to Watch

- Open rate on Email 1 (should be highest)
- Click rate on Email 2 (engagement signal)
- Reply rate if using forced engagement (deliverability signal)
