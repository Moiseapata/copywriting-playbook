# Email Cadence and Timing

**Source:** Alex Hormozi, industry data
**Tags:** #email #cadence #timing #optimization

## The Ideal Cadence

**Sweet spot: 3 times per week**

This is the consensus from top email marketers. It keeps you present without overwhelming.

## The Quality Threshold (Hormozi)

Don't commit to a cadence that forces low-quality work. Commit to a **quality threshold:**

- If you have two good ideas, send two
- If you have four, send four
- If you have zero, send zero

**Better to send less often with great content than frequently with filler.**

## The Batch Solution

If starting is the problem, batch the work.

**Hormozi's approach:** "I sat down for half a day and wrote 24 emails—a full year's supply for a twice-a-month newsletter. The barrier was not time, but just doing it."

## Best Send Times

### By Business Type
- **B2C:** Mondays and Tuesdays (+18% open rates)
- **B2B:** Wednesdays (similar lift)

### By Time of Day
- **Sweet spots:** 10:00 AM to noon, and 1:00 PM to 3:00 PM
- **Avoid:** Early mornings (competitive with high-priority business emails)

## The "Deposit Before You Withdraw" Principle

Think of subscriber trust as a bank account:
- Every value email is a deposit
- Every promotional email is a withdrawal
- You must maintain a positive balance

The ratio isn't fixed, but the principle is: provide consistent value and only occasionally make an ask.

## Long-Term Thinking

Only ~15% of buyers convert within the first 90 days. The majority of revenue comes later. Optimize for long-term trust, not short-term conversions.
