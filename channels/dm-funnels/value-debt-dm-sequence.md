# Value Debt DM Sequence

**Source:** Alex Hormozi (synthesized from $100M Leads and case studies)
**Tags:** #dm #outreach #value-debt #hormozi

## Core Concept

The most powerful DM strategy puts prospects in "value debt" before ever asking for a sale. You give so much free value that they feel compelled to engage.

## The Psychology

When someone receives a genuinely valuable free gift, they feel a psychological debt. The weight of that free value makes them feel obligated to at least reply, click, or consider the offer.

## The Sequence

### Step 1: Identify the Right People
- Dream 100 list (Brunson): The top people, communities, and accounts where your ideal customers already gather
- Engage with their content genuinely before DMing

### Step 2: The Value-First DM
Do NOT pitch. Give something genuinely useful for free.

**Example:** "I noticed you're doing [X]. I put together a quick breakdown of how [similar account] grew from 1K to 50K followers using a specific content format. Want me to send it over?"

### Step 3: Deliver Massive Value
Send the resource. Make it better than most paid stuff.

### Step 4: Follow Up With a Question
After they've consumed it: "Did you find that useful? What part stood out?"

### Step 5: The Natural Transition
Only now, after value is established: "I actually help people implement this exact strategy. If you ever want to explore what that would look like for you, let me know."

## Key Principles

### Give Away Your Best Stuff
"Give away a $9,500 course for free." The free content should be so good people would have paid for it.

### Make It Easy to Say Yes
Start with free or heavily discounted: "I'm taking on five case studies. Do you know anyone who..."

### The "Learning Stage" Mindset
When starting, you're in the learning stage, not the earning stage. Offer free work to get case studies, then raise prices.

### The Pricing Ramp
1. Free (first 5 clients — learning stage)
2. 80% off (next 5 — building proof)
3. 60% off (next 5 — growing demand)
4. Full price (established)
