# Voice of Customer Research Methods

**Source:** Andre Chaperon, Peep Laja, Tyson 4D
**Tags:** #research #voice-of-customer #interviews #chaperon

## Core Principle

Understanding the audience is the most important factor in effective copy. Without it, even good stories fail. Research is more important than writing.

## Research Hierarchy (Best to Worst)

### 1. Direct Interviews (Highest Value)
One real conversation reveals more than 100 survey responses.

**How to do it:**
- Call or video chat with customers
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen for exact language and phrases
- Record emotional moments
- "What was the worst moment before you found our solution?"

### 2. Surveys Inside Email Sequences
Embed questions in your email flow when engagement is high.

**Example:** Send a simple email asking one question. Can generate dozens of detailed responses.

### 3. Casual Email Questions (Reply-Based)
"Hit reply and tell me: what's your number one struggle with [topic] right now?"

### 4. Forum and Community Observation (Baseline)
Watch where your audience gathers. Note:
- Common questions
- Repeated frustrations
- Exact language they use
- What solutions they discuss (and dismiss)

### 5. Live Chat Transcripts and Support Tickets
Real problems, real language, real objections.

## What to Listen For

### Pain Language
The exact words people use to describe their struggles. These become your headlines.

### Desire Language
How they describe the outcome they want. These become your promises.

### Objection Language
What they worry about, doubt, or fear. These become your FAQ and objection-handling copy.

### Transformation Language
How they describe "before" and "after." These become your testimonials and case studies.

## Chaperon's Process

1. Identify audience pain, desires, and goals
2. Map the desired transformation
3. Create story-driven content that guides readers step-by-step
4. Align emotional triggers inside the narrative

## When Traffic or Customer Volume Is Low

Work harder to reach the few people you do have:
- Call them directly
- Follow up personally
- Make feedback part of the customer experience
- Even a small number of real conversations can reveal the core problem quickly

## The Key Insight

"Without understanding the audience, even good stories fail."
