# Pain Extraction

**Source:** Alex Hormozi
**Tags:** #research #pain #testimonials #hormozi

## The Core Question

Don't ask: "How was life before [your product]?"

Ask: **"What was the WORST moment?"**

## Why This Works

General questions produce general answers. Specific questions about specific moments produce vivid, emotional, usable copy.

### Generic vs. Specific

**Question:** "How was business before?"
**Answer:** "Things weren't going great." (Useless)

**Question:** "What was the worst moment?"
**Answer:** "We were two months away from shutting our doors. I had to tell my wife we might lose the house." (Powerful)

## How to Extract Pain

### In Interviews
1. "Take me back to before you found [solution]. What was a typical day like?"
2. "What was the moment you realized something had to change?"
3. "What was the worst that ever got?"
4. "What did you try before that didn't work?"
5. "What did it cost you—not just money, but in other areas of your life?"

### In Surveys
- "Describe the lowest point before you found us."
- "What were you most afraid would happen if nothing changed?"

### In Testimonial Requests
- "What was the worst moment before you started? Be specific—I want to feel what you felt."

## Using the Extracted Pain

Pain language becomes:
- **Headlines:** "Two Months From Shutting Our Doors..."
- **Email subject lines:** "The moment I almost lost everything"
- **Ad hooks:** "If you've ever had to tell your spouse you might lose the house..."
- **Testimonial copy:** Vivid before-and-after contrasts

## The Hormozi Testimonial Framework

After reviewing 2,500 testimonials, the top performers all had one thing in common: a **pain-based hook.**

Structure:
1. Worst moment (specific, vivid)
2. Discovery of solution
3. Implementation process
4. Specific result
5. Current state

**Example:** "We were two months away from shutting our doors. Then we found [method]. We implemented [specific action]. Within 90 days, we went from $0 profit to $47,000/month. Now we're at $1.2M/year and I sleep at night."
