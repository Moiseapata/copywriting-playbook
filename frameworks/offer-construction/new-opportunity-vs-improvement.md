# New Opportunity vs. Improvement Offer

**Source:** Russell Brunson
**Tags:** #offer-construction #positioning #brunson

## Core Concept

Most offers fail because they're positioned as improvements on things that already failed. Winners position as entirely new opportunities.

## The Two Types

### Improvement Offer (Avoid This)
- Ends in "-er": faster, cheaper, easier
- Problem: Customer already tried that path and failed
- You're selling a "better way to fail"
- Conversion rates: 2-3%

**Example:** "Lose weight faster with our new diet pill"

### New Opportunity Offer (Use This)
- Positioned against everything the market has tried
- "That old way is broken and painful. Here's a completely new vehicle."
- Conversion rates: 18-20%

**Example:** "The old diet model is why you've failed. Here's a completely different approach to metabolism that has nothing to do with calories."

## The Psychology

When someone has tried and failed multiple times, they don't want a better version of what didn't work. They want something that feels fundamentally different—a new hope.

## How to Apply

1. Identify what your market has tried and failed at
2. Name those failures explicitly
3. Position your solution as categorically different
4. Explain WHY the old way is broken (not just that it didn't work for them)
