# Hook, Story, Offer

**Source:** Russell Brunson
**Tags:** #hook-story-offer #diagnosis #brunson #core-framework

## Core Concept

Every single component in a funnel—every ad, landing page, email, webinar—must have a hook, a story, and an offer. If a funnel is broken, one of these three elements is off. This is the universal diagnostic tool.

## The Three Components

### 1. Hook
**Job:** Get attention. Stop the scroll.
**Must do:** Create enough curiosity to move into story.
**Does NOT need to:** Sell anything, explain anything, or provide value.

**Formats:**
- Curiosity headline
- Dramatic visual
- Counterintuitive statement
- Pattern interrupt

### 2. Story
**Job:** Change the emotional state of the prospect. Shift beliefs.
**Must do:** Make the prospect feel understood and show a new possibility.
**Key insight:** Story is not decoration—it's a persuasion tool.

**Types of stories:**
- Origin story (how you discovered the thing)
- Vehicle story (why the method works)
- Internal story (why they can do it)
- External story (why outside obstacles can be overcome)

### 3. Offer
**Job:** Close the sale. Convert belief into action.
**Must do:** Feel like the natural, irresistible next step.
**Positioning:** Not a product—a specific, framed solution.

## The Diagnostic Rule

**If something isn't converting, ask:**
- Is the hook not stopping attention? → Fix the hook
- Is the story not shifting beliefs? → Fix the story
- Is the offer not closing? → Fix the offer

**The answer is almost always in one of these three.**

## How to Use

Run the diagnostic on every element:
- Ad → Hook strong? Story engaging? Offer clear?
- Landing page → Hook above fold? Story in body? Offer in CTA?
- Email → Hook in subject line? Story in body? Offer in CTA?
- Webinar → Hook in first 5 min? Story in first 15? Offer in close?
