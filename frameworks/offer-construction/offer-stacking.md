# Offer Stacking (Every Solution Creates a New Problem)

**Source:** Russell Brunson
**Tags:** #offer-construction #value #stacking #brunson

## Core Concept

The best offers don't just solve one problem—they anticipate and solve the cascade of problems each solution creates. A complete offer is not a product; it's a kit.

## The Cascade

1. **Solution 1:** You give them the map (the strategy/framework)
2. **New Problem:** "I need software to build this" 
3. **Solution 2:** Include the software/templates
4. **New Problem:** "I need copy for the software"
5. **Solution 3:** Include copy templates/swipe files
6. **New Problem:** "I need traffic"
7. **Solution 4:** Include traffic training/sources

## The Analogy

"Don't just give them a map to Mount Everest and let them die on the climb. The offer must be a complete kit: the map, the Sherpa, and the gear."

## How to Apply

1. Start with your core product
2. Ask: "Once they have this, what's the next thing they'll struggle with?"
3. Solve that too
4. Repeat until the offer feels comprehensive
5. Stack the value in your copy: "You get A ($X value), B ($Y value), C ($Z value)... total value $XX, your price $Y"

## The Pricing Effect

The more value you stack, the more the price feels like a fraction of the whole. This is how you compete on value instead of price.
