# Visual, Falsifiable, Unique (Harry Dry's 3 Rules)

**Source:** Harry Dry
**Tags:** #copy-rules #clarity #differentiation #harry-dry

## Core Concept

Strong copy passes three tests. If a sentence fails any of them, it's weak. If it passes all three, it's probably great. This is the simplest, most practical copy filter available.

## The Three Rules

### 1. Can I Visualize It?

Abstract language is weak. Concrete language is strong.

**Weak:** "Change an entire industry"
**Strong:** "New Balance worn by supermodels in London and dads in Ohio"

**The Fix:** Zoom in on every phrase and ask what it actually means. Rewrite vague statements until they become something visible and specific.

**Examples:**
- "Regain fitness" → "Getting off the couch and running 5K"
- "A better way" → [Something you can actually picture]
- "Seamless transition" → [Nobody can picture this]

**The Test:** Close your eyes. Can you see it? If not, rewrite it.

### 2. Can I Falsify It?

A sentence should be something that could be proven true or false. This makes writing feel grounded and credible.

**Weak:** "He's funny and smart"
**Strong:** "Looks like Ryan Gosling. Reads on the tube. 6 foot 2."

**The Principle:** Point at evidence instead of leaning on vague adjectives. Show the graph, the object, the result, or the proof.

### 3. Can Nobody Else Say It?

Your best copy should be unique to you. If a competitor could swap their logo onto your page and everything still fits, you haven't differentiated.

**Weak:** "We care about quality"
**Strong (Volvo):** "Your car has five numbers on the speedometer. Volvo has six."

**Why This Matters:** Strong positioning is exclusionary. What only you can say is what makes you distinct.

## The Combined Test

Run every important line through all three:
1. Can I picture it? (Concrete)
2. Could someone prove it wrong? (Credible)
3. Could a competitor say it? (Distinctive)

If you pass all three, the copy is strong.

## The Standard

"If you can't add or remove anything without making it worse, the piece is done."
