# Open Loops

**Source:** Andre Chaperon
**Tags:** #curiosity #storytelling #email #engagement #andre-chaperon

## Core Concept

An open loop is an unresolved story element that creates a curiosity gap. The human brain is wired to seek resolution. When you start a story without finishing it, the reader feels psychological tension until they get closure. This is the mechanism behind serialized storytelling and high email engagement.

## How Open Loops Work

1. **Start a story** without immediate resolution
2. **Create a curiosity gap** (incomplete information)
3. **Reader feels tension** until closure
4. **Promise resolution** in the next email/video/installment

## Example

**Email 1:** "Something strange happened to me the other day at the airport..."

**Email 2:** "So I'm at the airport, and this guy approaches me..."

**Email 3:** "Here's what the stranger at the airport taught me about marketing..."

## Why It Works for Email

- Readers actively anticipate the next email
- Open rates stay high across sequences
- Engagement becomes habitual
- The narrative carries the marketing message naturally

## Chaperon's TV Show Analogy

Your email sequences should work like episodes of a TV show (e.g., Lost). Each episode:
- Delivers value within the narrative
- Resolves some threads
- Opens new questions
- Makes viewers anticipate next week

## Key Rule

**You must eventually close the loop.** Unresolved curiosity that never pays off destroys trust. The resolution must be genuinely satisfying.

## How to Use

1. Plan your sequence with open loops in mind
2. End each email with a teaser for the next
3. Close loops within 1-3 emails
4. Open new loops as you close old ones
5. Weave the product/offer naturally into the narrative resolution
