# Chain of Beliefs (Buckets Framework)

**Source:** Andre Chaperon
**Tags:** #psychology #belief-shifting #pre-framing #andre-chaperon

## Core Concept

Prospects have a chain of existing beliefs. You cannot force a new belief until you've addressed the links that come before it. Each "bucket" represents a belief that must be established before the next one can be accepted.

## The Framework

### Bucket 1: Problem Bucket
Define the pain clearly in their language. If they don't believe they have a problem, nothing else matters.

### Bucket 2: Naming
Assign an identity they accept. "Oh, that's me. I'm a Frank."

### Bucket 3: Pivot
Introduce the transformation point. "But here's what I discovered..."

### Bucket 4: Solution Bucket
Position the product as the bridge.

## How to Use

Before presenting any offer, map out:
1. What does the prospect currently believe about their problem?
2. What do they believe about available solutions?
3. What do they believe about their ability to solve it?
4. What would they need to believe to buy?

Then create content that shifts each belief in sequence. **Never jump to the solution before the problem is fully felt.**
