# Self-Selection Strategy

**Source:** Andre Chaperon
**Tags:** #segmentation #email #trust #andre-chaperon

## Core Concept

Instead of blasting your entire list with every offer, let subscribers self-select into interested segments. This dramatically increases engagement, reduces list fatigue, and builds trust by respecting attention.

## The Mechanism

### Step 1: The Interest Email
Send ONE email to your full list introducing an offer. Include a link that says something like:
"Click here if you're interested in learning more about [topic]"

### Step 2: Tag the Interested
Only people who click get tagged as interested.

### Step 3: Follow Up Only with Interested
Subsequent emails about this offer ONLY go to the interested segment.

## Why This Works

- **Higher conversion:** You're selling to interested people
- **Less fatigue:** Uninterested subscribers aren't bombarded
- **More trust:** You respect their attention
- **Better data:** You learn what segments of your list care about

## Chaperon's Insight

"You don't need to hit the entire list over the head to sell something."

A small, highly engaged segment often outperforms blasting the full list.

## The "Raise Your Hand" Principle

Throughout your email strategy, constantly invite subscribers to raise their hand:
- "Reply YES if you want me to cover this"
- "Click here if you're struggling with X"
- "Let me know if this resonates"

Each response is a signal you can use to segment and personalize.

## How to Apply

1. Before any promo sequence, send a single interest-gathering email
2. Tag clickers as interested
3. Only send the full promo to the interested segment
4. Everyone else continues receiving value content
5. Re-test interest periodically (people change)
