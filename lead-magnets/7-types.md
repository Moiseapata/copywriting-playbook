# The 7 Types of Lead Magnets

**Source:** Amy Porterfield
**Tags:** #lead-magnets #formats #porterfield

## Core Distinction

The "topic" (what you teach) is different from the "format" (how you deliver it). Choose the format that best fits your content and your audience's consumption preferences.

## The 7 Types (Simplest to Most Advanced)

### 1. Cheat Sheet (or Checklist)
**Definition:** A jam-packed series of pages with steps, examples, and visuals. Designed to be skimmed for quick wins.

**Pros:** Quick read, easy to digest, eye-catching
**Cons:** Doesn't go deep; if your topic requires detailed explanation, this isn't the format

**Example:** "20 Smart Strategies to Rapidly Grow Your Email List"

### 2. Workbook
**Definition:** Information plus interactive elements like fill-in-the-blanks and guided exercises. 3-10 pages max.

**Pros:** High perceived value because it's actionable; opportunity to share resources (including affiliate links)
**Cons:** More investment of time to create and for customer to complete

**Warning:** A 40-page workbook will be saved to a folder and never opened. Keep it short.

### 3. Guide
**Definition:** More of a narrative where you lead the customer through a story and stack value. Meatier than a cheat sheet but not too long.

**Pros:** Immensely valuable; well-done guide makes people say "I can't believe this is free"
**Cons:** Takes time to create; fine line between valuable and too long

**Example:** "17 Tiny Tweaks That Produce Big Results" (James Wedmore)

### 4. Video or Audio Training
**Definition:** Direct-to-camera video or private audio (15-20 minutes) that delivers the free content.

**Pros:** Higher perceived value, deeper connection, conversational
**Cons:** More work and potential cost; if uncomfortable on camera, don't force it

### 5. Challenge (Advanced)
**Definition:** A 5-10 day experience with a specific daily action. Use email delivery, not Facebook groups.

**Pros:** Huge list builder (likely the biggest of all seven types); highly shareable; you become the daily cheerleader
**Cons:** Requires serious strategy, time, and focus. Not for beginners.

**Example:** "5 Days to Find Hidden Money" (Rachel Cruze)

### 6. Quiz (Advanced)
**Definition:** Interactive tool where users answer questions and get personalized results.

**Pros:** High-converting (people love learning about themselves); you gain valuable audience data from answers
**Cons:** Tricky to create and set up; requires smart technology; results must feel genuine and specific

**Tool:** Interact (Amy's recommendation)

### 7. Free Course (Mini-Course)
**Definition:** A 2-3 module sneak peek, not a full academy. Provides an "aha" moment but leaves them wanting more.

**Pros:** Showcases teaching talent; provides massive "aha" moments
**Cons:** Biggest risk: satisfying the customer just enough that they don't feel the need to buy. Must carefully balance value.

**Example:** "Permission to Prosper" (Ray Edwards)

## The 24-Hour Rule

Choose your lead magnet type within 24 hours. Don't overthink it. Once you know the type, you likely already know the content.

## For Beginners

Stick to options 1 or 2: Cheat Sheet, Checklist, or Workbook.
