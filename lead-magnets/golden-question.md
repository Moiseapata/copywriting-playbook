# The Golden Question

**Source:** Amy Porterfield
**Tags:** #lead-magnets #strategy #alignment #porterfield

## The Question

Before creating any lead magnet, ask:

**"What does my ideal customer avatar need to understand, be aware of, or believe in order to eventually want or need my paid product, program, or service?"**

## Why This Matters

Most lead magnets fail because they're disconnected from the eventual sale. They provide value but don't create a path to revenue.

Your lead magnet starts a conversation. Your weekly content continues it. Both lead the prospect toward a buying decision.

## The Alignment Principle

The lead magnet must serve as a logical primer for the paid offer.

### Example 1: List Building Course
- **Paid offer:** List Builders Society (course on building a profitable email list)
- **Needed belief:** Building an email list is vital AND doable for me
- **Lead magnet:** "20 Smart Strategies to Rapidly Grow Your Email List" (Cheat Sheet)
- **Why it works:** It shows WHAT to do (empowering). The paid course teaches the specific HOW.

### Example 2: Weight Loss Membership
- **Paid offer:** Weight loss membership site (not based on food plans)
- **Needed belief:** The method is NOT about food—it's about mindset and principles
- **Lead magnet:** "Three Things You Must Know to Lose Weight" (Free Mini-Course)
- **Why it works:** Teaches principles upfront. Filters out people who only want calorie counting.

## What NOT to Create

The wrong lead magnet starts the conversation in the wrong place.

**Bad Example for List Building Course:** "How to Choose an Email Service Provider"
- Too technical, overwhelming, unsexy
- Addresses a necessity but scares beginners
- Better saved for inside the paid course

**Bad Example for Weight Loss Membership:** "How to Meal Prep to Lose Weight"
- Starts a conversation about food
- Attracts wrong customer
- Makes it harder to shift their mindset later

## The Action Step

Ask the Golden Question. Answer it honestly. Then build your lead magnet to bridge exactly that gap.
